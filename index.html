Overview

About the Course

Introduction to Engineering Design is the foundational PLTW engineering course. Students work through the full product development cycle — from initial sketches through parametric 3D models to fabricated prototypes — using industry-standard tools and practices.

The course places heavy emphasis on Autodesk Inventor for solid modeling, and on the discipline of technical documentation that makes engineering work reproducible and communicable. Students learn not just how to build, but how to record their thinking so others can build too.

  • Create orthographic and isometric technical sketches by hand
  • Build parametric 3D models and assemblies in Autodesk Inventor
  • Apply geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) to drawings
  • Reverse-engineer an existing product and document its components
  • Fabricate a prototype and evaluate it against design criteria

Curriculum

Units

Unit 1

Design Process & Documentation

Engineering notebooks, design briefs, iteration logs, and technical communication standards.

Unit 2

Technical Sketching

Orthographic projection, isometric drawing, section views, and freehand proportion.

Unit 3

3D Solid Modeling

Autodesk Inventor: sketching, extrusion, features, assemblies, and drawing sheets.

Unit 4

Geometric Dimensioning & Tolerancing

GD&T basics, tolerance stack-up, fits, and communicating precision in drawings.

Unit 5

Reverse Engineering

Disassemble, measure, model, and document a real product's components and function.

Unit 6

Prototype Fabrication

3D printing, post-processing, testing against design criteria, and iteration.

Unit 7

Culminating Design Project

Student-driven design challenge with full documentation, CAD model, and prototype.
